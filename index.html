Featured work

USA TODAY

Copy, Paste, Legislate

Published: April 2019

Art directed and designed the EPPY award-winning USA TODAY Network series, “Copy, Paste, Legislate”, that found more than 10,000 bills nationwide that were copied almost verbatim from models written by special interest groups. This collaborative project involved teams from USA TODAY, The Arizona Republic and Center for Public Integrity. The online story had more than 525,000 page views in the first week.

Ahead of the Fire

Published: July 2019

Collaborated with USA TODAY graphics and The Arizona Republic to design, art direct and develop the project “Ahead of the Fire,” which is a deep-dive data analysis of wildfire hazards across 760 million acres along the Western coast of America. Greenville News

Taken

Published: January 2019

Designed the project “Taken,” an exclusive report by Greenville News, about civil asset forfeiture and its prominence in South Carolina black communities. As a result of the project, the South Carolina state legislature moved forward with a bipartisan bill that would require due process before law enforcement can seize an individual’s property.

Indianapolis Star

Fix This Now

Published: April 4, 2019

Conceptualized and designed the Indianapolis Star’s first front-page editorial condemning Indiana’s Religious Freedom Restoration Act that did not include protections for the LGBTQ community. Under extreme deadlines and design limitations, created a dynamic front page that went viral. Within a week of publication, then-Gov. Mike Pence signed a new law protecting the LGBTQ community.

Cincinnati Enquirer

Newspaper Redesign

Published: April 4, 2019

Redesigned the Cincinnati Enquirer’s broadsheet newspaper to create a unique style that better fit its new compact paper size. The redesign focused on creating a news magazine style that introduced bolder visuals and more dynamic alternative story forms. Designed a new logo that was used in both print and online publications. Responsible for creating a comprehensive style guide that included display typography, section colors and theming, and paragraph styles. Also responsible for creating and art direction marketing campaigns focused on informing the reader about the changes made to the newspaper.

Other shit I do

Develop training program

Created a new Design Studio training curriculum to meet quarterly and yearly goals of increasing designer’s involvement in online story projects Training focused on the proprietary platforms and frameworks for online story projects. Ran and instructed weekly training classes that consisted of 20+ designers in each class. So far in 2019, over 30 designers have completed online projects, up from 5 in 2018.

Develop new features

Work with product developers to add new features and functionality to digital storytelling frameworks that will enhance the reader’s experience and offer new ways to tell stories more effectively. This process includes feature conceptualization, developer handoff and VQA.

Project management

Project manage enterprise and investigative stories with newsrooms by collaborating with writers, photographers, designers, and social media. Responsible for developing the story’s overall art direction, project roadmap and schedule, and coordinating design and development between multiple departments. Completed projects with The Arizona Republic, Asbury Park Press, Louisville Courier Journal, Indianapolis Star, Greenville News, Rochester Democrat and Chronicle, Westchester News and Journal.

Microsoft Teams Migration

Lead the team responsible for transitioning the Design Studio’s primary source of communication from Skype/email to Microsoft Teams. Responsible for creating workflows that would enable studio Studio designers to communicate with Gannett’s 110 newspapers in areas concerning daily and weekly production. Within a month, created, coordinated and instructed 40+ training classes for all 300+ Design Studio employees and 500+ newsroom employees.

Training news designers

Created and instructed a series of classes for studio Studio designers that focused on a variety of news design topics including graphic design fundamentals, illustration, art direction, and photo editing. The training classes meet the department’s yearly goal to offer employees more opportunities for career development.

Transition new papers into the Studio

Part of the management team responsible for transitioning the daily of design and production from the newsrooms to the Design Studio. The transition included quickly building a relationship with the newsroom and assisting the editors and reporters in switching to a new content management system. Successfully oversaw the transfer of the Indianapolis Star, Lafayette Journal & Courier, Muncie Star Press, Richmond Palladium-Item, York Daily Record, Hanover Evening Sun, Chambersburg Public Opinion, Lebanon Daily News.
